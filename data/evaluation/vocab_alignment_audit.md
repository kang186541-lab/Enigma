## Step 1: Curriculum Vocabulary Summary

### Unit 1 (Day 1-6): First Encounters
**Key vocabulary taught:**
- **Greetings:** Hello, Nice to meet you, How are you today, Good morning
- **Introductions:** My name is..., I'm from..., I live in..., What's your city like?
- **Jobs:** What do you do?, I'm a student, I work at a company, Do you speak English?, A little bit, detective (Day 6 review)
- **Farewells:** Goodbye, See you later, Take care, It was nice meeting you
- **Survival phrases:** Thank you, Sorry/I don't understand, Help/Please help me, Yes/No, Can you say that again please?, Can you speak more slowly please?, Where is the bathroom?, Excuse me where is the exit?, How much is this?

### Unit 2 (Day 7-12): Everyday Expressions
**Key vocabulary taught:**
- **Numbers:** one through one hundred, fifteen dollars, compound numbers
- **Age:** How old are you?, I'm twenty-five years old
- **Days:** Monday-Sunday, weekend, What day is it today?, from Monday to Friday, See you on Wednesday
- **Time:** What time is it?, It's three o'clock, half past seven, meeting at ten in the morning, wake up at seven
- **Weather:** How's the weather?, sunny, cloudy, raining, cold, hot, snowing, windy, warm, cool
- **Colors/Sizes:** red, blue, green, yellow, white, black, big/small, large, medium, light/dark, What color is...?

### Unit 3 (Day 13-18): Food & Ordering
**Key vocabulary taught:**
- **Food preferences:** I like pizza/pasta, What food do you like?, I love Korean food, I don't like spicy food, Are you hungry?
- **Ordering:** Can I have the menu please?, I'd like a coffee please, Can I have water please?, I'll have the chicken please, For here or to go?
- **Prices:** How much is this?, It's ten dollars, That's too expensive!, Can I pay by card?, The bill please
- **Food feelings:** This is delicious!, It's very good, It's too salty/sweet, I'm full. Thank you!
- **Recommendations:** I recommend the steak, You should try the soup, What do you recommend?, The salad is very fresh, Let's eat together!

### Unit 4 (Day 19-24): Places & Directions
**Key vocabulary taught:**
- **Places:** Where is the subway station?, The cafe is near the museum, Is there a pharmacy nearby?, How far is it?, five minutes on foot
- **Directions:** Turn left/right, Go straight ahead, It's on your left/right, Take the first street on the right, Cross the bridge, next to the convenience store, At the intersection turn right, Take exit three from the subway
- **Transportation:** Can I get a taxi please?, To the airport please, How much to the city center?, One ticket to Seoul Station please, Does this bus go to the museum?
- **Getting help:** Excuse me I'm lost, Can you show me on the map?, I don't know this area, Which way is the train station?, Is this the right way to the museum?

### Unit 5 (Day 25-30): People & Social
**Key vocabulary taught:**
- **Family:** This is my family, I have two brothers and one sister, My mother is a teacher, My father works at a hospital, Do you have any children?
- **Describing people:** She is tall/has long hair, He is wearing a blue jacket, My friend is very kind, The man with glasses is my teacher, She looks young but she is thirty
- **Hobbies:** What do you like to do in your free time?, I like reading books and watching movies, Do you play any sports?, I play football every weekend, My hobby is cooking
- **Feelings:** How are you feeling today?, I am very happy today, I am tired because I worked all day, Are you okay? You look worried, I am excited about the trip!
- **Making plans:** Are you free this weekend?, Let's meet at the cafe at three o'clock, Would you like to go to the cinema?, Sorry I can't I have plans, That sounds great! See you there

### Architectural Issue

The root cause is that **story quizzes were designed for narrative engagement rather than vocabulary reinforcement**. The thematic words (editor, conspiracy, frequency, activate, beneath, memory, mother tongue) serve the mystery plot but are not connected to the training camp curriculum.

**Recommended structural fix:** Each quiz should be rebuilt with this formula:
- **80% of tested words** must be from the allowed day range in the curriculum
- **20% maximum** may be guessable new words (cognates, context clues)
- **0%** should be advanced abstract vocabulary (conspiracy, frequency, activate)

The story narrative can still use ANY vocabulary in dialogue/narration. The constraint applies ONLY to quiz answers and options that the player must actively produce or select.
